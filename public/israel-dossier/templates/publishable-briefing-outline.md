## 8. Draft Structure

1. One-sentence claim boundary.
2. Verified public-record floor.
3. Reported humanitarian figures with attribution.
4. Aid ledger and record-type distinctions.
5. Incident evidence with disputed elements visible.
6. Legal record and procedural posture.
7. Open questions and next verification steps.

## 9. Editor QA

- Every number traces to a source row.
- Every legal term matches procedural posture.
- Every reported figure is attributed.
- Every estimate is labeled as an estimate.
- Every incident uses source-supported verbs.
- No protected class, religion, ethnicity, nationality, or ancestry is used as evidence of wrongdoing.
- Open questions remain visible.
- Downloaded source copies or archive fallbacks are attached.
