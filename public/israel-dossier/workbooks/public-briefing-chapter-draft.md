# Israel Dossier Chapter Draft - Source-Boundary Edition

Last source check: 2026-04-22

Working title: Public Records, Reported Harm, and Legal Posture

## Editorial Boundary

This draft is built from the populated Israel dossier workbooks. It is not a final legal finding, not a casualty adjudication, and not a substitute for source review. Each section preserves source-row IDs so an editor can trace numbers, incident language, and legal posture back to the workbook file before publication.

## 1. The Financial Floor

Source rows: SRC-P-001, AID-P-001, AID-P-002, AID-P-003, AID-P-004

Paragraph source IDs: SRC-P-001, AID-P-001, AID-P-002

The financial record begins with a public-record floor. The Congressional Research Service identifies Israel as the largest cumulative recipient of U.S. foreign assistance since World War II and estimates inflation-adjusted U.S. aid obligations at $298 billion from 1946 through 2024. CRS separately reports current-dollar assistance and missile-defense totals in a different accounting frame. These figures should not be merged or treated as interchangeable.

Paragraph source IDs: AID-P-003, AID-P-004

The recent appropriations record should also remain distinct. H.R.815 added a $26.4 billion Israel security supplemental in April 2024, including missile-defense and procurement categories. The 2016 memorandum of understanding supplies a separate annual baseline for FY2019 through FY2028. These records establish scale, continuity, and congressional action. They do not, by themselves, prove delivery timing, end use, or direct causation in any named incident.

Unsafe wording to avoid: Do not write that a specific death or incident was paid for by a specific appropriation unless a delivery record, weapon-transfer record, and end-use record establish that chain.

## 2. Reported Humanitarian Harm

Source rows: HUM-P-001, HUM-P-002, HUM-P-003, HUM-P-004, HUM-P-005, HUM-P-006

Paragraph source IDs: HUM-P-001, HUM-P-002, HUM-P-003

The humanitarian record is substantial, but the source classes are not identical. OCHA reported 72,289 Palestinian fatalities in Gaza as of 1 April 2026, attributed to MoH Gaza. UNICEF reported 21,289 children killed and 44,500 children injured through 3 February 2026. Those are reported figures with named attribution chains and date ranges.

Paragraph source IDs: HUM-P-004, HUM-P-005

Survey estimates need their own label. A Lancet Global Health survey estimated 75,200 violent deaths through 5 January 2025; that figure should be described as a survey estimate, not a live administrative count. OHCHR reported that women and children made up 70 percent of verified fatalities in homes and residential buildings in its sample; that sample boundary has to remain visible.

Unsafe wording to avoid: Do not strip attribution from reported figures, and do not present a survey estimate, a monitor tracker, and an administrative report as one undifferentiated count.

## 3. Incident Evidence

Source rows: INC-P-001, INC-P-002, INC-P-003, INC-P-004, INC-P-005

Paragraph source IDs: INC-P-001, INC-P-003

The incident file can support narrative detail if the evidence type stays attached to each sentence. Forensic Architecture reconstructed the killing of Hind Rajab and reported evidence consistent with fire at close range. That supports forensic-reconstruction language. It does not, without a competent legal finding, establish individual criminal liability.

Paragraph source IDs: INC-P-002, INC-P-004, INC-P-005

World Central Kitchen said seven aid workers were killed after its convoy was struck in Gaza. OCHA reported recovery of bodies from PRCS, Palestinian Civil Defense, and UN personnel in Rafah after the March 2025 paramedic convoy killings. OCHA also reported a UNRWA school sheltering displaced people was struck in An Nuseirat Refugee Camp in June 2024. Each event should remain its own row, with its own source, disputed elements, and safe wording.

Unsafe wording to avoid: Do not convert a reconstruction, press investigation, aid-organization statement, or UN incident report into a final adjudication of intent or individual liability.

## 4. Legal Posture

Source rows: LAW-P-001, LAW-P-002, LAW-P-003, LAW-P-004, LAW-P-005

Paragraph source IDs: LAW-P-001, LAW-P-002, LAW-P-003

The legal record is publishable only if procedural posture remains visible. The ICJ ordered provisional measures in South Africa v. Israel; provisional measures are not a final merits ruling. The ICJ advisory opinion on the occupied Palestinian territory stated that Israel's continued presence is unlawful; the advisory-opinion posture remains part of the fact being reported. ICC public records describe warrants in the Situation in the State of Palestine; a warrant is not a conviction.

Paragraph source IDs: LAW-P-004, LAW-P-005

UN Security Council and General Assembly records belong in the legal and diplomatic record, but they should not be collapsed into court findings. A court order, advisory opinion, prosecutor action, warrant, Security Council resolution, and General Assembly vote each carry a different legal and political meaning.

Unsafe wording to avoid: Do not write that a court has finally adjudicated genocide, guilt, or individual conviction unless the cited record says that.
