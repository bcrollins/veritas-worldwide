# Israel Dossier Briefing Draft - Source-Boundary Version

Last source check: 2026-04-22

## Claim Boundary

This briefing documents a source-backed record of U.S. aid, reported humanitarian harm, selected incident evidence, and legal proceedings connected to Israel, Gaza, and the occupied Palestinian territory. It does not ask the reader to treat every source class as the same kind of proof. Public records, reported figures, survey estimates, forensic reconstructions, court orders, warrants, advisory opinions, and analysis are separated because each supports a different level of certainty.

## Verified Public-Record Floor

The financial floor begins with the Congressional Research Service. CRS identifies Israel as the largest cumulative recipient of U.S. foreign assistance since World War II and estimates inflation-adjusted U.S. aid obligations to Israel at $298 billion from 1946 through 2024. CRS separately reports $174.965 billion in current-dollar assistance and missile-defense funding through 2025. These figures should not be merged. The constant-dollar figure is an inflation-adjusted estimate. The current-dollar figure is a separate accounting frame. Neither figure proves, by itself, that a specific U.S. domestic program was displaced.

The recent appropriations record should also remain separate. H.R.815 added a $26.4 billion Israel security supplemental in April 2024, including missile-defense and procurement categories. That law is an appropriation record, not a delivery log and not an incident record. DSCA notices are proposed arms-sale records unless delivery and end use are separately documented.

## Reported Humanitarian Figures

OCHA reported 72,289 Palestinian fatalities in Gaza as of 1 April 2026, attributed to MoH Gaza. UNICEF reported 21,289 children killed and 44,500 children injured through 3 February 2026. These are reported figures with named source chains and date ranges. They should remain attributed in prose.

Survey estimates require a separate label. A Lancet Global Health survey estimated 75,200 violent deaths in Gaza through 5 January 2025. That is a survey estimate, not a live administrative count. OHCHR reported that women and children made up 70 percent of verified fatalities in homes and residential buildings in its sample; that sample boundary must remain visible.

## Incident Evidence

Incident language should use the lowest defensible certainty level. Forensic Architecture reconstructed the killing of Hind Rajab and reported evidence of fire at close range. That supports forensic reconstruction language, not individual criminal liability unless a court or named investigative authority supplies that finding.

World Central Kitchen said seven aid workers were killed after its convoy was struck in Gaza. OCHA reported recovery of bodies from PRCS, Palestinian Civil Defense, and UN personnel in Rafah after the March 2025 paramedic convoy killings. OCHA also reported a UNRWA school sheltering displaced people was struck in An Nuseirat Refugee Camp in June 2024. Each event needs its own source row, disputed-elements note, and safe wording before it moves into narrative form.

## Legal Record

Legal posture matters. The ICJ ordered provisional measures in South Africa v. Israel; provisional measures are not a final merits ruling. The ICJ advisory opinion on the occupied Palestinian territory stated that Israel's continued presence is unlawful; advisory-opinion posture remains part of the record. ICC public records describe warrants in the Situation in the State of Palestine; a warrant is not a conviction.

## Open Questions

The next editorial pass should attach exact paragraph references, archive fallbacks, and source copies for every high-risk sentence. Any claim about intent, motive, individual liability, or final legal classification should remain out of public copy until the source row supports that level of certainty.
